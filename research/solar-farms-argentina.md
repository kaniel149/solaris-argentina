*Sources: PV Magazine (Feb 2026), PV Tech, CAMMESA data*

*Source: PVKnowHow, Ken Research*

### Utility-Scale vs Distributed Generation

- **Utility-scale (>10 MW):** ~90% of installed solar capacity. Dominated by MATER market and RenovAr projects
- **Distributed generation (<2 MW / PMGD):** ~10%. Growing under Law 27.424 but still small
- **Mid-scale (2-10 MW):** Emerging via RenMDI program (3-20 MW projects)

**Best regions for utility-scale solar: Cuyo (established, best infrastructure) and NOA (highest irradiation, proven with Cauchari)**

### Solar Irradiation Data

- **NOA (Jujuy/Salta Puna):** 2,200-2,400 kWh/m²/yr GHI — comparable to Atacama (Chile), among best globally
- **Cuyo (Mendoza/San Juan):** 1,800-2,200 kWh/m²/yr — excellent, comparable to southern Spain
- **Optimal tilt (Cuyo, ~33°S):** ~29° north-facing
- **Capacity factors achieved:** 25-30% in Cuyo/NOA (high for LatAm)
- **LCOE potential (NOA):** Below USD 30/MWh

*Sources: Global Solar Atlas, Solargis, BSW Solar Argentina Report*

## 2. Regulatory Framework

### Law 27.191 — Régimen de Fomento de Energías Renovables

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Enacted** | 2015 (Macri government) |
| **Target** | 20% renewable electricity by December 31, 2025 |
| **Achievement** | ~16.5% average in 2024; peaks >20% in some months |
| **Fiscal stability** | **EXPIRED December 31, 2025** |
| **Extension attempt** | Failed in 2025 legislature. Industry now seeking 12-month bridge in 2026 budget |
| **Tax incentives** | Accelerated depreciation, VAT refunds, import duty exemptions — all expired |
| **Current status** | Law still exists but fiscal benefits gone. No updated targets (government rejected raising to 30%) |

**Critical point:** The expiry of Law 27.191's fiscal stability provisions creates regulatory uncertainty for 2026+. The government under Milei prefers market-driven approaches over subsidies.

*Sources: Energía Estratégica, Strategic Energy, The Energy Pioneer*

### MATER Market (Mercado a Término de Energías Renovables)

The MATER has become **the primary vehicle for new solar development**, replacing government auctions.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What it is** | Private bilateral PPA market between generators and large consumers |
| **Who participates** | Generators, large industrial users (>300 kW demand), banks, commercial users |
| **How it works** | Direct contracts, no government subsidies. Higher margins than regulated market |
| **Current pipeline** | **136 projects, 6,020 MW** prioritized |
| **Full MATER** | 3,727 MW — no injection limits |
| **Referential A** | 2,293 MW — up to 8% curtailment pending grid upgrades |
| **Solar share** | ~39% of installed solar capacity contracted through MATER |
| **Recent award** | 365 MW in latest MATER round (2025/2026), solar-led |

**Key insight:** MATER is where the action is. Corporate buyers (mining, industrial, banking) are driving demand. This is the market Solaris should target.

*Sources: Strategic Energy, Shale24, Net Zero Circle*

### CAMMESA Role & PPA Structure

- **CAMMESA** (Compañía Administradora del Mercado Mayorista Eléctrico): manages the wholesale electricity market (MEM)
- Administers RenovAr PPAs and dispatch
- Sets grid connection requirements and priority
- **Resolution 400/2025:** Mandates 75% forward contracting via PPAs, recognizes BESS, enables spot market for renewables
- **Resolution SE 21/2025:** Allows operational renewable generators to enter PPAs and spot contracts freely

### Grid Connection Requirements

- Apply to **CAMMESA** for dispatch priority and MEM participation
- **Transener** manages high-voltage transmission (500 kV backbone)
- Connection studies required (technical feasibility, grid impact)
- **Major bottleneck:** Transmission infrastructure is severely underdeveloped
  - Years of underinvestment
  - 16 priority transmission projects announced (5,600+ km) — private concession model
  - Remote solar regions (NOA, Cuyo) far from demand centers (Buenos Aires)
  - Some MATER projects accept 8% curtailment due to grid constraints

### Environmental Permits (EIA)

- **Provincial jurisdiction** — each province has its own EIA process
- Typically requires: environmental impact study, public hearing, provincial environmental authority approval
- Timeframe: 6-18 months depending on province
- Municipal permits also required (land use, construction)
- No federal-level solar-specific EIA framework

### Tax Incentives — Current Status (2026)

| Incentive | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Accelerated depreciation | **EXPIRED** (was under Law 27.191) |
| VAT refunds on equipment | **EXPIRED** |
| Import duty exemptions | **EXPIRED** |
| Fiscal stability guarantee | **EXPIRED** — industry seeking 12-month bridge |
| RIGI regime | **ACTIVE** (enrollment until July 2027) — but requires >$200M investment |
| Provincial incentives | Vary by province — limited for solar specifically |

**RIGI (Régimen de Incentivo para Grandes Inversiones):**
- For projects >$200 million investment
- Tax breaks, import exemptions, export tax relief
- 30-year fiscal stability
- FX benefits (20-100% foreign currency access over 4 years)
- International arbitration available
- Enrollment extended to July 7, 2027
- **Already approved:** El Quemado (305 MW, $211M, YPF Luz)
- **Limitation:** Too large for most new entrants. Designed for oil/gas/mining scale

*Sources: Curtis law firm, Strategic Energy, State Dept Investment Climate Statement*

## 5. Opportunities & Challenges

### Where Is the Market Heading?

1. **MATER dominance:** Government auctions are over. Private PPAs are the future
2. **BESS integration:** AlmaGBA (713 MW awarded), AlmaSADI (700 MW tender) — hybrid solar+storage is the next wave
3. **Market liberalization:** Resolution 400/2025 mandates forward contracting, enables spot market for renewables
4. **Doubling ahead:** Solar forecast to grow from 2.5 GW → 4.3 GW by 2029
5. **Corporate demand:** Mining (lithium), industrial, banking sectors driving private PPAs
6. **Green hydrogen:** Long-term opportunity, but pre-FID stage. Mostly wind-driven in Patagonia

### Room for New Entrants?

**Yes, but with caveats:**
- Market is consolidating around Genneia, YPF Luz, Central Puerto, AES
- Mid-tier developers (50-200 MW) have room — see Coral Energía (20 MW → 1 GW target)
- Solar DQD shows EPC → developer pathway
- MATER enables smaller independent developers
- RenMDI specifically designed for 3-20 MW projects
- **Key barrier:** Need local partnerships for land, grid, permitting

### Feasible Project Sizes

| Segment | Size | Market Mechanism | Entry Barrier | Opportunity |
|---------|------|-----------------|---------------|-------------|
| **PMGD / Distributed** | <2 MW | Law 27.424, net billing | Low | Limited revenue, good for learning |
| **RenMDI scale** | 3-20 MW | CAMMESA auction | Medium | Proven path, 44 projects awarded in 2023 |
| **Mid-scale MATER** | 20-100 MW | Private corporate PPA | Medium-High | Sweet spot for new entrants |
| **Utility-scale** | 100+ MW | MATER / RIGI | High ($100M+) | Dominated by established players |

### PMGD (Pequeño Medio de Generación Distribuida)

- Under Law 27.424 (Distributed Generation)
- Projects up to 2 MW capacity
- Net metering/billing compensation
- FODIS fund provides financing support
- Provincial jurisdiction for grid connection
- Growing but still ~10% of market
- Good starting point for market entry

### Hybrid Projects (Solar + Storage)

- **AlmaGBA tender (2025):** 713 MW BESS awarded, $540M investment
- **AlmaSADI tender (2026):** 700 MW BESS nationwide, bids open
- Solar + BESS increasingly required for grid stability
- Examples: Solar DQD (20 MW solar + 15 MWh BESS), Coral Energía BESS projects
- **Premium positioning** — higher value than standalone solar

### Green Hydrogen Connection

- Government aims to be major exporter by 2030
- 143 projects in South American pipeline worth ~$263 billion
- Currently pre-FID stage — macro uncertainty (FX, inflation, policy)
- Mostly wind-driven (Patagonia), but solar possible in NOA
- Long-term play — 5+ year horizon

### Corporate PPA Trend

**This is the strongest trend in Argentine solar:**
- Mining companies (lithium in NOA/Cuyo) need reliable clean power
- Industrial users seeking cost savings and ESG compliance
- Banks and large commercial users participating in MATER
- Resolution SE 21/2025 enables free PPA contracting for generators
- 75% forward contracting mandate (Resolution 400/2025) drives demand

### Main Challenges

| Challenge | Severity | Detail |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Grid capacity** | 🔴 Critical | Insufficient transmission from solar regions to demand centers. 5,600 km of new lines planned but years away |
| **Regulatory uncertainty** | 🔴 High | Law 27.191 fiscal benefits expired. No replacement. RIGI only for mega-projects |
| **Currency/FX risk** | 🟡 Medium-High | Historical peso devaluation. Milei stabilization improving but not resolved. PPAs denominated in USD help |
| **Political risk** | 🟡 Medium | Milei government pro-market but unpredictable. 2026 elections (Oct 26) may shift priorities |
| **Inflation** | 🟡 Medium | Declining from hyperinflation but still elevated. Impacts local costs |
| **Financing** | 🟡 Medium | International finance available but cautious. Local banks limited. RIGI improves bankability for large deals |
| **Import logistics** | 🟢 Low-Medium | Equipment importation works but duty exemptions expired |

## 6. Entry Strategy for Solaris Argentina

### Recommended Approach: Phased Entry

#### Phase 1: PMGD / Small-Scale (0-12 months)
**Target:** 1-5 projects, 0.5-2 MW each

- Enter through distributed generation (Law 27.424)
- Lower capital requirement ($0.5-2M per project)
- Learn the regulatory landscape, build local relationships
- Target: industrial rooftops or small ground-mount near consumption
- Revenue via net billing + surplus injection

#### Phase 2: Mid-Scale MATER (12-24 months)
**Target:** 1-2 projects, 10-50 MW each

- Develop pipeline for MATER corporate PPA market
- Target mining companies, industrial users in NOA/Cuyo
- Partner with established EPC (Solar DQD or similar)
- Secure land options in Mendoza or San Juan
- Total investment: $7-40M per project

#### Phase 3: Utility-Scale (24-36 months)
**Target:** 50-150 MW projects

- Leverage Phase 2 track record
- Consider hybrid solar + BESS for premium value
- Potential RIGI qualification for projects >$200M
- Partner or co-develop with established developer

### Recommended Project Size

**Sweet spot: 10-50 MW (MATER corporate PPA)**

Reasons:
- Below RIGI threshold — no need for $200M minimum
- MATER market is active and accessible
- RenMDI precedent (3-20 MW projects awarded)
- Manageable capital ($7-40M range)
- Corporate PPA offtake available
- Solar DQD builds 600 MW/year — EPC capacity exists

### PMGD vs Utility-Scale vs Corporate PPA

| | PMGD (<2 MW) | Corporate PPA (10-50 MW) | Utility-Scale (100+ MW) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Capital** | $0.5-2M | $7-40M | $70-150M |
| **Timeline** | 6-12 months | 12-24 months | 24-36 months |
| **Risk** | Low | Medium | High |
| **Returns** | Moderate | Good | Best at scale |
| **Recommendation** | Start here | **Main target** | Phase 3 |

### Partnership Needs

1. **Local legal counsel** — essential for EIA, CAMMESA registration, provincial permits (e.g., PAGBAM or similar)
2. **EPC partner** — Solar DQD (largest, 600 MW/yr capacity) or emerging firms
3. **Land partner** — secure land rights in Mendoza or San Juan (Cuyo = best infrastructure + excellent irradiation)
4. **Grid connection advisor** — CAMMESA/Transener requirements specialist
5. **Offtake partner** — target lithium miners, industrial groups, or utilities for MATER PPA
6. **Finance partner** — IFC/IDB for larger projects; local banks for smaller

|-----------|-------|

| Land identification & option | 2-4 |

### Investment Required

| Phase | Project Size | Capital per Project | Total Phase Investment |
|-------|-------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Phase 1 (PMGD) | 0.5-2 MW | $0.5-2M | $1-5M |
| Phase 2 (MATER) | 10-50 MW | $7-40M | $15-80M |

**Minimum viable entry:** ~$1-2M (single PMGD project)
**Recommended initial commitment:** $5-10M (2-3 PMGD + one mid-scale development pipeline)

### Recommended Target Regions

1. **Mendoza (Cuyo)** — #1 choice
   - Largest solar cluster (1,095 MW installed)
   - Proven infrastructure (Genneia, YPF Luz active here)
   - Good irradiation (1,800-2,200 kWh/m²)
   - Accessible terrain, lower altitude than NOA

2. **San Juan (Cuyo)** — #2 choice
   - Central Puerto active (Hunuc I pipeline)
   - Mining demand (lithium, gold)
   - Strong irradiation
   - Aisa 1 GW project signals investor confidence

3. **Salta/Jujuy (NOA)** — for premium irradiation
   - Best irradiation in Argentina (2,200-2,400 kWh/m²)
   - Cauchari proving ground
   - More remote, infrastructure challenges
   - Lithium mining PPA opportunities

## Key Takeaways for Solaris Argentina

1. **Timing is favorable:** Solar is booming — 810 MW added in 2025 alone, market projected to double by 2029
2. **MATER is the mechanism:** Government auctions are dead. Corporate PPAs drive everything now
3. **Regulatory gap exists:** Law 27.191 expired, creating uncertainty but also opportunity for agile players
4. **Start small, grow fast:** PMGD for learning → MATER for revenue → utility-scale for growth
5. **Cuyo is the place:** Mendoza/San Juan offer the best combo of irradiation, infrastructure, and demand
6. **Partnerships are essential:** Cannot enter alone — need local EPC, legal, land, and offtake partners
7. **BESS is the differentiator:** Hybrid solar+storage projects command premium and grid priority
8. **$5-10M gets you in the door** with a credible development pipeline
