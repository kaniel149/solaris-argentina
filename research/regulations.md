# Regulatory Framework — Solar Argentina
## Updated March 2026

---

## Key Laws & Regulations

### Ley 27.191 (2015) — Régimen de Fomento de Energías Renovables
- **Status:** EXPIRED end of 2025. Extension sought via 2026 budget
- **What it did:** Set 20% renewable target, provided tax exemptions, subsidies, RECs
- **Impact:** Mobilized $8B+ in investment, 7,133 MW installed
- **Current situation:** Industry (CEA) pushing for fiscal stability clause in 2026 budget
- **Our position:** Framework exists even without formal extension. FODIS and I-RECs still active.

### Ley 27.424 (2017) — Generación Distribuida
- **Status:** Active
- **What it does:** Allows prosumers to inject surplus energy to grid
- **Key provisions:**
  - Net billing (not net metering — credits at wholesale rate, not retail)
  - Systems up to 300 kW for commercial, 500 kW industrial
  - Bidirectional meter required
  - Zero injection device alternative (no grid export)
- **Important:** Zero injection is simpler (no utility approvals needed)

### FODIS — Fondo para la Generación Distribuida
- **Status:** Active but severely underfunded
- **What it does:** Loans, incentives, guarantees for distributed generation
- **Budget 2025:** 310M ARS allocated, 0 utilized
- **Our opportunity:** Even underfunded, FODIS provides legal backing for financing

### FODER — Fondo para el Desarrollo de Energías Renovables
- **Status:** Active
- **What it does:** Long-term loans, credit lines, financial guarantees
- **Relevance:** More for utility-scale, but supports distributed indirectly

### MATER — Mercado a Término de Energías Renovables
- **Status:** Active, 450 MW awarded
- **What it does:** Bilateral PPA market for large users (>300 kW demand)
- **Relevance:** Our larger C&I clients can participate

### Resolución SE 400/2025
- **Status:** New framework
- **What it does:** Market-oriented bilateral contracts
- **Relevance:** Opens up more flexible PPA structures

## Green Certificates

### I-REC (International Renewable Energy Certificates)
- **Issuer in Argentina:** IRAM (since February 2021)
- **How it works:** 1 MWh generated = 1 I-REC
- **Value:** Tradeable certificate proving renewable energy origin
- **Use cases:**
  - Scope 2 emissions reduction (GHG Protocol)
  - RE100 compliance
  - CDP reporting
  - ESG marketing
- **Process:** Register plant with IRAM → I-TRACK system → issue certificates
- **Revenue:** Additional income stream ($1-3 per MWh estimated)

### Carbon Credits
- I-RECs are NOT carbon credits
- But they indirectly support decarbonization claims
- Argentina has no formal carbon market yet
- International voluntary markets accessible

## Grid Connection

### For Zero Injection Systems (simpler):
1. Install zero injection device (included in Intermepro quote)
2. No utility approval needed
3. All generated energy consumed on-site
4. **Recommended for first projects** — fastest path

### For Net Billing Systems (grid export):
1. Apply to local utility (Edenor/Edesur in Buenos Aires area)
2. Install bidirectional meter
3. Utility approval process: 30-60 days typical
4. Surplus credited at wholesale rate
5. Annual settlement

### Generator User Permit (Habilitación de Usuario Generador):
- Required for systems injecting to grid
- Provincial authority approval
- Licensed engineer must certify
- **Not included in Intermepro quote** — additional cost
