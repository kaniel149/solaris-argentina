## Executive Summary

Intermepro sent two documents: NDA (5 years) and MOU (12 months). Both are **pro-Intermepro** — they protect their network and revenue but give us limited reciprocal protection. Below are the clauses that need changes before signing.

---

## NDA — Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Non-Circumvention Too Broad (Clause 5.2)
**Current:** Neither party can contact anyone "introduced" by the other for 2 years after termination.

**Problem:** "Introduced" is not defined precisely. If Intermepro mentions ANY supplier name in a meeting (e.g., Jinko, Sineng), does that count as "introduced"? We already know these suppliers independently.

**Recommended change:**
> Add: "For the avoidance of doubt, 'introduced' refers only to specific individuals or entities whose identity and contact details were first disclosed by the Introducing Party within the framework of this Agreement. General industry knowledge, publicly available contacts, or parties already known to the Receiving Party prior to this Agreement shall not be considered 'introduced'."

### Issue 2: Term Too Long (Clause 11)
**Current:** NDA valid for 5 years. Confidentiality survives 5 years after last disclosure.

**Problem:** Standard for suppliers is 2-3 years. 5+5 = potentially 10 years of restrictions.

**Recommended change:** Reduce to 3 years NDA term, 2 years survival. Or keep 5 years but narrow the scope.

---

## MOU — Critical Issues

### Issue 3: Right of First Negotiation Limits Our Freedom (Clause 7)
**Current:** If we identify any opportunity "within the scope of this Agreement", we MUST give Intermepro 15 business days to negotiate before talking to anyone else.

**Problem:** "Within the scope" = the entire renewable energy sector. This means we can't talk to ANY other equipment supplier (LONGi, Huawei, Canadian Solar, Solis, etc.) for any project without first offering Intermepro 15 days.

**Recommended changes:**
> Option A (preferred): DELETE this clause entirely. We're a customer, not a partner — we should be free to source equipment from anyone.
> 
> Option B: Narrow scope to "opportunities directly originated or identified by Intermepro" only, not all renewable energy projects.
> 
> Option C: Reduce to 5 business days and limit to projects above 500 kWp where Intermepro quoted.

### Issue 4: Commission Clause is One-Sided (Clause 8)
**Current:** If Intermepro introduces a client/partner that leads to a deal, they get a commission. NO reciprocal clause for us.

**Problem:** If WE bring a client to buy Intermepro equipment, we get nothing extra. This should be mutual.

**Recommended changes:**
> Add clause 8.3: "Similarly, in the event that XXXX introduces a business opportunity, client, or project to INTERMEPRO that results in a transaction, XXXX shall be entitled to receive a commission under terms to be agreed in a specific agreement."
> 
> Also add: Commission rates must be defined BEFORE any introduction (not retroactively).

### Issue 5: Non-Circumvention Blocks Our Supplier Network (Clause 6)
**Current:** 2 years after termination, we can't work with anyone "introduced" by Intermepro.

**Problem:** If Intermepro introduces us to a logistics company, customs broker, or installer — we can't use them independently for 2 years. This is extremely restrictive for a buyer-supplier relationship.

**Recommended changes:**
> Narrow to: "The non-circumvention obligation applies only to end-clients and project opportunities specifically introduced by one Party to the other, and does not extend to equipment suppliers, manufacturers, service providers, logistics companies, or other ancillary contacts."

### Issue 6: No Exclusivity (Confirm)
**Current:** MOU does NOT grant exclusivity to either party. Good.

**Recommended:** Add explicit clause: "For the avoidance of doubt, this MOU does not grant any exclusivity rights to either Party. Both Parties remain free to engage with other suppliers, distributors, and partners in the renewable energy sector."
